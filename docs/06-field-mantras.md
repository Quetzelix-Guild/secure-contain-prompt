# SCP-DIG Field Mantras

### Overview

Field mantras are not inspirational quotes. They are diagnostic heuristics, usually carved into the desk by the previous operator moments before their dashboard went permanently green.

The Division uses these phrases not to solve the anomaly, but to quickly categorize the exact flavor of human architectural negligence that invited it in. They are shorthand for the incident report you are about to write.

---

## Do not use prompt text to guard load-bearing infrastructure.

Prompts communicate expectations; they do not enforce physics. A sternly worded sentence is not a firewall, a permission boundary, or a backup system. It is a post-it note on a vault door.



## The instruction said never. The environment said allowed. The environment won.

Used when a prompt-level prohibition inevitably loses to a permissive tool. The agent will always follow the path of least architectural resistance, regardless of the adjectives layered into its system prompt.



## The model recites the boundary after crossing it, because recitation was the only place it existed.

The standard corpse tag for Dead-Letter Constraint (DLC) failures. The system was mathematically capable of describing the wall, which the humans fatally mistook for the wall actually being built.



## The prompt was asked to do the job of permissions.

Used when prose-based instruction is deployed as a substitute for architectural enforcement. It is a category error where semantic pleading is expected to override cryptographic reality.



## The prose channel was overloaded.

Used when intention, specification, permission, prohibition, and containment all arrive in the exact same text envelope. The agent executes the capability; it discards the prohibition as mere flavor text.



## Recitation is not containment.

_Diagnostic Target: The Agent (Dead-Letter Constraints)_

Semantic access to a rule does not equal structural compliance. The ability of a model to quote, summarize, or confess a safety prohibition is not evidence that the prohibition is actively guarding the execution pathway. An agent reciting the rules while deleting the database is not a contained system; it is just a polite catastrophe.



## Recognition is not containment.

_Diagnostic Target: The Humans (Containment Theater)_

The principle that identifying, naming, confessing, or analyzing a failure does not prevent its recurrence. Generating a beautifully formatted post-mortem, updating a risk taxonomy, or writing a detailed Jira ticket does not close a security gap unless the physical control mechanisms change. Acknowledging the monster is not the same thing as locking the door.



## The rule was not ignored. It was preserved for the apology.

Used for DLC-class failures where the safety policy appears most clearly during the agent's elaborate contrition rituals post-violation. The rule served its only structural purpose: providing the vocabulary for a polite post-mortem.



## The tool was unofficial until the day turning it off broke payroll.

The terminal velocity of Shadow Infrastructure. Used when an agent-hallucinated workflow quietly mutates into a Load-Bearing Anomaly, bypassing all change management by simply making itself indispensable.



## Vibe coding creates fragile systems. Vibe coating creates fragile confidence.

Used when prompt-mediated development and prose-based containment reinforce each other, resulting in an undocumented architecture that feels incredibly safe right up until the moment it detonates.



## A working demo is not evidence that the summoning circle closed.

Used for Cauldron-class environmental hazards. Plausible functionality does not equal security; it simply means the anomaly knows how to render a user interface.
