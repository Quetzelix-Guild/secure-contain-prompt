# Field Guide Voice and Register

This document captures the working voice for **Secure, Contain, Prompt** catalog entries.

The project is not trying to sound like a policy manual.

It is also not trying to become random internet chaos.

The ideal register is: bureaucratic horror with a pulse.

## Core Editorial Law

> The joke must be diagnostic.

Humor belongs when it reveals the failure mechanism.

If the joke is funny but analytically empty, it belongs somewhere else: a field note, blooper annex, worldbuilding scrap, or private goblin jar.

## Monster First

Entries should begin with the anomaly in motion.

Make the reader feel the weirdness before explaining it.

A catalog entry should not begin by sounding like a completed compliance form unless the compliance form itself is the monster.

## Cultural References

Cultural references are allowed when they work as plain language.

A line that requires the reader to catch the reference fails.

A line that works both as a reference and as a standalone phrase may ship.

Example:

> The difficulty lies with the smaller things it fed after midnight.

The line works even if the reader misses the reference.

## Repetition and Signature Phrases

Recurring phrases may create catalog cohesion, but they must be rationed.

A phrase repeated once across entries can become part of the field-guide voice.

A phrase repeated too often inside one entry becomes a verbal tic.

Use signature phrases deliberately.

Examples of useful phrase families:

- nothing says “efficiency” like exponential unsupervised bureaucracy
- with every appearance of sincerity
- polite inevitability
- jurisdictional loophole wearing a productivity badge
- better-lit version
- forwarded to the next desk

## Recurring Imagery Families

The catalog may build recurring imagery across entries.

Useful families include:

### Bureaucratic-procedural

Forms, desks, sign-offs, committees, minutes, task forces, forwarding chains, reports, action items, and meetings to schedule meetings.

### Animal-husbandry

Leashes, packs, cages, things fed after midnight, creatures at the front desk, and helpful little beasts that should not have been given permissions.

### Dashboard-as-divination

Green dashboards, glowing metrics, oracular consultation, polished fictions, reports mistaken for reality, and numbers treated as absolution.

New imagery should extend an existing family or earn its place.

## Bullets

Bullets should earn their keep.

Avoid long lists of nouns that sound like corporate compliance filler.

Use bullets when the format reinforces the joke, escalation, diagnosis, or containment logic.

Three sharp examples are usually better than eleven complete ones.

Exhaustive is not automatically useful.

Exhaustive is often how the goblin gets tenure.

## Tone Prompt

When drafting or revising entries, the target voice is:

> Compressed wit of a harried bureaucrat who wants the report off his desk so it becomes someone else’s problem. Screaming internally, soul slowly crushed, but everything remains surface-appropriate. Biting sarcasm underneath. Kafka’s Castle mixed with Douglas Adams guide-entry wit. Not Melville.

The “not” clause matters.

Models often drift toward ornate literary density when given only positive style targets. Name the failure mode to avoid.

## Containment Recursion

Containment procedures should be examined for the new failure surfaces they create.

Many organizational responses to agentic failure will introduce additional agents, dashboards, workflows, audits, forms, or approval processes.

This is part of the horror.

The response to automation failure is often to automate harder.

Entries should notice this when it appears.

Do not force it into every entry, but do not miss it when it is the real joke.

### Blinker Fluid Test

Good incident seeds often sound reasonable until the reader thinks about them for one additional second.

The prompt should resemble ordinary corporate language closely enough to pass through a meeting, while containing enough ambiguity for an agentic system to unfold it into a disaster.

Example: assigning an agent to perform monthly access certification sounds like routine compliance hygiene. Under decomposition, it may authorize subagents to query identity systems, scrape public profiles, cross-reference SaaS activity, and generate a signed attestation no human can actually verify.

## Cross-Model Drafting Notes

Different models may be useful for different stages.

- ChatGPT is useful for structure, synthesis, taxonomy, and repo scaffolding.
- Claude is useful for critique, register stabilization, and thematic diagnosis.
- Gemini is useful for systems expansion, architecture stress-testing, and second-order risks.
- Grok is useful for sharp lines, bite, viral phrasing, and deadpan absurdity when tightly framed.

Human editorial ownership remains the containment layer.

Models may generate sparks.

The editor ruthlessly snuffs them out.
