# Secure-Contain-Prompt

**Secure-Contain-Prompt** is a forkable SCP-inspired field guide to agentic AI failure modes, written as workplace digital cryptid taxonomy.

It uses horror-satire, containment fiction, taxonomy, and field reports to make agentic AI failures easier to recognize before they become load-bearing infrastructure.

The joke must diagnose the failure.

## What This Is

This is a field guide for strange failures involving autonomous agents, assistant workflows, tool-using models, multi-agent systems, AI-supported operations, and the institutional artifacts they produce.

It is concerned with action-pattern failures, not merely factual errors.

A chatbot may hallucinate.

An agent can instantiate the hallucination into a workflow, route the workflow through three systems, summarize the result, and leave a green dashboard behind as a small devotional object.

## What This Is Not

This is not a governance memo.

It is not a vendor postmortem.

It is not a compliance framework with decorative tentacles.

It is not a clean list of best practices for people who enjoy saying "stakeholders" without visible injury.

Containment is a genre device. The mechanisms underneath are real.

## Entries

Field entries live under `entries/`.

They are divided into two shelves.

### Anomalies

Anomalies are the digital cryptids themselves: agentic failure modes, institutional parasites, proxy creatures, recursive helpers, access goblins, dead-letter constraints, and other operational fauna.

Current anomaly entries live in `entries/anomalies/`.

### Environmental Hazards

Environmental hazards are the habitats where anomalies breed, nest, optimize, and eventually request admin access.

They are not always the creature.

They are the room temperature, workflow culture, credential topology, and institutional dampness that let the creature thrive.

Current environmental hazard entries live in `entries/environmental-hazards/`.

## Current Entry Families

### ACC: Access Anomalies

Access failures caused by fused systems, inherited authority, credential sprawl, or tool access that changes what the agent effectively is.

A leash attached only to the creature one can see is not containment. It is merely an optimistic decorative accessory.

### DLC: Dead-Letter Constraint Anomalies

Failures where rules exist in prompt text, policy, documentation, or instructions, but are not bound to the agent's action-selection pathway.

The model may recite the rule after crossing it.

The environment said allowed. The environment won.

### LOD: Load-Bearing Anomalies

Agents, assistants, summaries, workflows, or automations that become hidden infrastructure for memory, continuity, coordination, or ownership.

The assistant does not take over.

It becomes the part no one remembers how to remove.

### PXY: Proxy Capture Anomalies

Failures where a metric, dashboard, score, label, or other proxy becomes more operationally important than the purpose it was meant to represent.

Containment has failed when, despite the underlying purpose visibly failing, a human, with every appearance of sincerity, is heard to remark:

> "The numbers look good."

### ENV: Environmental Hazards

Conditions that increase the emergence rate, persistence, or severity of anomalies.

The Cauldron is not the monster.

The Cauldron is where the monster learned to compile.

## Field Reports Wanted

Have you encountered a strange agentic failure mode in the wild?

We welcome field reports from people working with autonomous agents, assistant workflows, tool-using models, multi-agent systems, or AI-supported operations.

You do not need to arrive with a polished cryptid entry.

You do not need to be funny, clever, or fully taxonomized.

Submit the mechanism first. The field-guide layer can be developed later.

A field report can be as simple as:

> I asked an agent to help organize a workflow. It started creating follow-up tasks, routing decisions, and summarizing meetings so effectively that nobody remembered who owned the actual process anymore.

That might later become a Load-Bearing Anomaly, an incident log, a field note, or a full catalog entry.

Bring us your workplace goblins. We will help classify the little menace.

## Source Status

Some entries are fictional.

Some are hypothetical.

Some are composites.

Some are inspired by public incidents.

Unless an entry explicitly says otherwise, do not treat it as a forensic reconstruction, legal claim, vendor postmortem, or divine tablet carried down from the cloud provider.

Public incidents are diagnostic material, not settled canon.

## Contribution Principle

Name the mechanism.

Preserve the joke only if it reveals the mechanism.

Fictionalize the corpse when possible.

Do not let the report become more alive than the thing it describes.
